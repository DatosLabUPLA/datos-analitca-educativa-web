Ninth Symposium on the Study of Mathematical Work (ETM9)
From 01/06/2026 to 05/06/2026, Mexico City, Mexico. Modality: In-person
Description:

The ÉTM meetings are international symposia organized as thematic working groups based on participant contributions. The symposium format fosters exchange among participants and helps create a community of researchers with shared interests.

To facilitate interaction and discussion, the number of participants per topic is limited. Each symposium topic is introduced by a plenary presentation that highlights, in particular, the achievements of previous symposia. For ÉTM9, total enrollment will be limited to 90 participants. Discussion among participants takes place in general plenary sessions and through participation in one of the thematic working groups, which will be specified in the second call for papers.

The thematic groups discuss and develop theoretical perspectives in didactics on mathematical work, as well as aspects related to signs, tools, discourse, control, and necessity in mathematical work, thus highlighting the key elements that influence mathematical practice, particularly AI and digital technologies. In the context of learning, the genesis and development of mathematical work are examined, focusing on the central role of the teacher, trainers, groups, and interactions in this process. Situations and tasks that contribute to the construction and development of mathematical work are also analyzed, highlighting their importance in the evolution of this fundamental discipline.

The symposium lasts 5 days and, like its predecessors, will be trilingual (English, Spanish, and French). Contributions (oral presentations and posters) may be made in any of these three languages. Oral presentations will be accompanied by an

electronic slide presentation, which must be in one of the other languages ​​of the

symposium.

Where: Plantel: Centro Histórico o Del Valle (por precisar)., Mexico City, Mexico

When: From 01/06/2026 to 05/06/2026

Fourth Study Days on Mathematical Work (JETM4) – Artificial Intelligence and Mathematical Work (JTEM4)
From 03/11/2025 to 05/11/2025, Paris, France. Modality: In-person
Description:

The use of a traditional instrument (including digital ones) supports the construction of meaning. The burden of proof remains with the individual and relies on a known theoretical framework shared by the scientific community. The integration of intelligent tools disrupts this balance, as AI takes over part of the discursive dimension and relies on an opaque framework, dependent on the database used to train the model. Integrating intelligent tools requires demanding work from the individual, encompassing semiotic, instrumental, and discursive aspects, in a continuous adjustment between established principles and ongoing experience.

The work initiated focuses on experiments integrating various AI tools into initial teacher training, in mathematics and didactics courses, in France, Chile, and Quebec. These experiments will be conducted in parallel within the different educational systems. The fourth annual conference on the Study of Mathematical Work will provide an opportunity to compare our analyses with the structural differences in teacher training among the participating countries. These cultural and contextual aspects are essential for supporting an ethical and responsible approach to guiding the use of AI-powered tools. Particular attention will be paid to the challenges of mathematics teacher training, curriculum development, and the role of digital technology, especially AI, in partner countries. These sessions will provide an opportunity to encourage joint presentations by researchers.

Where: 8 Place Aurélie Nemours, 75013 Paris, Paris, France

When: From 03/11/2025 to 05/11/2025

More Information: Contact: Laurent VIVIER: laurent.vivier@u-paris.fr

Seventh Symposium on the Study of Mathematical Work (ETM7)
From 27/06/2022 to 02/07/2022, Strasbourg, France. Modality: Hybrid
Description:

The ETM meetings are international symposia organized as working groups around topics in mathematics education and related fields, based on participants' presentations. The symposium format fosters exchange among participants and allows for the formation of a community of researchers with shared interests. The Symposium will last six days and will be trilingual, like previous ones (English, Spanish, and French). Presentations, both oral and poster, may be submitted in one of these three languages; oral presentations must be accompanied by an electronic slide presentation in one of the other two symposium languages. The meeting will be organized around four themes, and each contribution must fit within one of the proposed themes. The number of participants is limited to 25 per theme to facilitate interaction and discussion. Each theme of the Symposium will be presented in a plenary session, which will include a review of the achievements of previous symposia and will also include activities for exchanging ideas between themes.

The ETM 7 symposium is supported by the "Association pour le Développement et la Diffusion de la Recherche sur les Espaces de Travail Mathématiques (ADRETM)".

Where: 141 avenue de Colmar, Strasbourg, France

When: From 27/06/2022 to 02/07/2022

More Information: Official Page - Contact: Comité de organización etm7-organisation@googlegroups.com.
